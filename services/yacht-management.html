Operational yacht care

Not a price list. A management structure.

Yacht management depends on the yacht, the season, the crew, the marina and the level of readiness the owner expects. The goal is simple: keep the boat under control when the owner is away, before guests arrive and during technical periods.

ControlRegular checks, photos, issue lists and owner-readable reports.
CoordinationMarina, contractors, access, quotations, works and timing.
ReadinessPreparation before owner use, guest arrival, season launch or yard periods.

    Scope

    Choose the level of support

    The management model is split into a base monthly layer, optional monthly add-ons and one-time actions. This keeps the estimate readable and prevents recurring work from being mixed with event-based tasks.

    01

    Base monthly service

    Yacht control alongside

    Mooring lines, moorings, bad-weather control, ventilation, battery charging and washing at least once a month.

    Technical control

    Start-up of onboard units and machinery, working-mode checks and battery condition.

    Owner representation

    Communication with marina administration and service companies on behalf of the owner.

    Price depends on length, yacht type and crew participation.

    02

    Monthly add-ons

    Monthly add-on blocks: operational works, finance, administration and sailing control for sailing yachts.

    03

    One-time actions

    Event-based services for guest arrival, seasonal launch, winterization, emergency visit, yard period, safety review, tender preparation or provisioning.

    04

    Readable estimate

    The calculator separates base month, monthly add-ons, monthly total and one-time services.

    Physical onboard work

    Washing, opening, ventilating, checking lines and fenders, preparing cabins and basic onboard routines. If competent crew is onboard, part of this work may already be covered.

    Management and control

    Planning, contractor control, reporting, budget supervision, marina communication, technical schedule and owner representation. Crew can support the boat, but this layer still needs structure.

    FAQ

    Before we prepare a proposal

    Is the estimate binding?

    No. It is an approximate budgeting range. Commercial terms are agreed individually after reviewing the yacht and confirming the service scope.

    Why does crew participation change the estimate?

    Crew can cover part of the daily onboard work. The management company then focuses more on control, coordination and reporting.

    Do sailing yachts require a separate scope?

    Often yes. Rigging, sails, furling systems, winches and deck hardware may add a separate operational layer.

    Can you supervise contractors?

    Yes. Operational works can include contractor selection, task assignment, progress control and practical communication with the owner.
